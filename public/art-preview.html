Full 9-character grid — all with rounded silhouettes

Every character now uses rounded head corners (2×2 pixel knockouts top/bottom), jaw taper (3 stepped rows at chin), sloped shoulders (staircase collar), cheek shading gradient, protruding ears, and shaped noses with highlight/shadow.

What changed:
  • Head corners knocked out — 2×2 pixel cutouts on all 4 corners make the silhouette read as a rounded skull instead of a cube
  • Jaw taper — bottom 6 rows narrow from 40px wide to 28px wide in 3 pixel steps, giving chin shape
  • Sloped shoulders — staircase of 3 rows (72→60→48 px) creates a natural V-shape from shoulder to neck
  • Collar dip — outfit notches down 2 px at center to show the neck connecting properly
  • Nose shape — 3-tone pixel column (bbb/999/777) gives 3D form instead of flat square
  • Cheek gradient — 2 overlapping opacity layers on right side (not a hard 50% split) = soft side-lighting
  • Ear nubs — 2×8 pixel protrusions at mid-face level with 2px outline — stops head from looking like a floating box
  • Facial hair follows jaw — beard pixels now match the tapered chin silhouette instead of being a flat rectangle
  • Hat/cap curves — crown has 2-step rounded top, brim is wider at sides to wrap around skull
